Email 01 of 03 — First Contact

The Cold Outreach Email

Use when reaching out to a prospect for the very first time — sourced via LinkedIn, Companies House, Crunchbase, or Apollo.io. Goal: earn a reply and open a conversation, not close a sale. You are demonstrating intelligence and offering value, not pitching a product.

STEP 1
Research prospect
10 mins on LinkedIn + website
STEP 2
Find 1 specific insight
Competitor move or market signal
STEP 3
Personalise template
Name the company + competitor
STEP 4
Send Tue–Thu AM
9–11am local time optimal
Subject line options — A/B test these:
Quick observation about [Company]'s competitive position ~42% open rate
I noticed something about [Competitor] you might want to see ~38% open rate
3 growth gaps I spotted in [Industry] this week ~35% open rate

Email 02 of 03 — Follow-Up

The Warm Follow-Up Email

Send 4–6 days after the cold email with no reply. Most deals are won on follow-up, not first contact. The key rule: always add new value. Never send a naked "just checking in" — it signals desperation and lowers your perceived status. This email brings something new to the table.

Subject line options:
RE: [Company] — one thing I forgot to include Threads open well
Update on [Competitor] — thought you'd want to see this ~33% open rate

Sending Strategy

The 6 Rules That Make
These Emails Win.

01
Research before you write
Spend 10 minutes on their website, LinkedIn, and Companies House before sending. One specific observation about their market beats five generic compliments every time.
02
Name competitors specifically
Vague references feel lazy. Naming Wise, Zalando, or Tesco — whoever is actually in their space — signals Theos Analytics has done the homework before even being hired.
03
One ask per email, always
Never ask them to reply AND book a call AND check the portfolio. One CTA only. A 20-minute call. A yes or no. Multiple asks create indecision and silence.
04
Send Tuesday–Thursday AM
Monday inboxes are chaotic. Friday is wind-down mode. Tuesday 9am or Wednesday late morning consistently outperform all other send windows for B2B cold outreach.
05
Follow up exactly once
One follow-up after 5 days with new value. If still no reply, connect on LinkedIn and move on. Chasing beyond this lowers your perceived status and harms the Theos Analytics brand.
06
Lead with the portfolio names
ASOS, Unilever, Gore-Tex, Godrej, P&G — these names open doors. Drop them naturally in paragraph one. Social proof does 80% of the credibility work before they've even read the offer.
